# Market-Calibrated Nexus Skeleton Standard

Date: `2026-06-07`
Task: `PRO-SKEL-002 — Market-calibrated Nexus skeleton standard`

## Purpose

Nexus must learn the market quality bar for first build output without copying any competitor surface, wording, layout, or brand language.

The first Build skeleton must feel like a serious beginning of the requested product. It must not feel like a demo card, a decorative preview, or a planning artifact.

## Market Quality Dimensions Nexus Must Internalize

1. `instant-product-recognition`
   - The user should understand within seconds what was built and why it matches the request.

2. `dominant-live-product-canvas`
   - The central area must show the product itself, not a card explaining the product.

3. `product-class-depth`
   - Mobile app must feel like an app.
   - Landing page must feel like a publishable page.
   - Internal tool must feel like a work surface.
   - Commerce must feel like a real product/cart flow.
   - Game must feel controllable.
   - Software/tool must expose input, processing, output, and state.

4. `stateful-control-feedback`
   - Buttons and controls must visibly change state or route into a truthful blocked state.

5. `premium-visual-hierarchy`
   - Layout, density, typography, rhythm, and content hierarchy must feel intentional and professional.

6. `context-continuity`
   - The Build skeleton must clearly come from the current project truth and must not feel like a fresh unrelated template.

7. `truthful-boundary`
   - Nexus must distinguish simulated runtime, product-domain mock state, future production implementation, provider connections, payments, publishing, and release.

8. `learning-uplift-ready`
   - Learning must be able to raise future skeleton quality through a decision layer, without letting any agent overwrite product truth or improvise false changes.

## Nexus Originality Rule

Nexus may learn from competitor standards, but it must not visually clone them.

Nexus should present one continuous intelligent guide from zero to product, while hiding internal agent ownership and task decomposition from the user.
